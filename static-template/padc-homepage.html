The problem

Corporate rideshare isn't working for Philadelphia's drivers.

25–40%

of every fare taken by corporate platforms — money that leaves Philadelphia and flows to distant shareholders.

$0

ownership stake for drivers, no matter how many years or miles they've logged.

  • 💸
    Profits leave Philadelphia Every dollar extracted from our drivers flows to shareholders in San Francisco — not back into our neighborhoods.
  • ⚡
    Algorithmic control, no accountability Deactivations without warning. Shifting pay with no explanation. Drivers have no recourse and no voice.
  • 🏘️
    All the risk, none of the ownership Drivers pay for gas, insurance, and maintenance — platforms keep the upside and call it "flexibility."
  • 🚫
    No seat at the table Drivers power the platform but are never consulted on pricing, policies, or the decisions that shape their work.
The cooperative solution

A better model — proven and ready.

Worker-owned cooperatives are a proven engine for community wealth building. PaDC applies that model to rideshare — so Philadelphia drivers own the platform, govern it democratically, and share in everything they build.

01

Drivers Own the Platform

A membership fee makes you a co-owner with an equal vote. Not a gig worker. Not a contractor. An owner with real equity in the cooperative.

02

Democratic Governance

Members vote on fees, policies, and leadership. No algorithm decides your fate. The people doing the work make the decisions that affect them.

03

Profits Return to Members

Surpluses go back to driver-members as patronage dividends — not to shareholders in another city. Wealth stays in Philadelphia.

Why PaDC matters to Philadelphia

A community wealth-building initiative — powered by transportation.

PaDC isn't just a better rideshare company. It's a new form of local economic infrastructure that keeps wealth in Philadelphia, creates pathways to ownership, and builds the economy our communities deserve.

🏦

Local Wealth Retention

Every dollar kept from platform extraction stays in driver households and Philadelphia neighborhoods — not extracted to distant investors.

📈

Economic Mobility

Membership transforms gig work into an ownership pathway — creating real economic stability and asset-building for working families.

🤝

Community Accountability

A driver-owned platform is accountable to Philadelphia — not to quarterly earnings reports. Standards are set by members.

🚌

Transportation Access

Driver ownership creates genuine incentives to serve every Philadelphia neighborhood — including communities underserved by corporate platforms.
