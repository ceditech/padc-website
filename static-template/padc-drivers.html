Why drivers are joining

More money. More control. Real ownership.

PaDC isn't another platform. It's a cooperative you own. Here's what that actually means for your work, your income, and your future.

💰

Keep more of every fare

Corporate platforms take 25–40% of every ride. PaDC is structured to operate on a lower platform fee — meaning more of what riders pay goes directly to you, every single ride.

🏦

Build real equity

Your membership fee is an ownership stake — not a subscription, not a fee. As PaDC grows, your equity grows with it. This is how wealth gets built, not just earned hour by hour.

🗳️

Have a real vote

Every member has one equal vote on fees, policies, and leadership. No algorithm decides your deactivation. No executive changes your pay rate overnight. Members decide.

📊

See where your money goes

PaDC operates with full transparency. Members see the platform's financials, understand how fees are set, and vote on major decisions. No black boxes. No surprises.

🤝

Share in the cooperative's success

When PaDC generates a surplus, it returns to driver-members as patronage dividends — not to investors elsewhere. You helped build it. You share in what it earns.

🛡️

Be part of something lasting

PaDC is built to last — not to flip, not to exit. As a member-owner, you're part of building an economic institution that will serve Philadelphia drivers for generations.

Common questions

Answers for drivers

We know drivers have real questions. Here are honest answers to the ones we hear most.

Can I actually make more money with PaDC?→
PaDC is structured to operate on a lower platform fee than traditional rideshare companies, which means more of every fare stays with you. Members also receive patronage dividends when the cooperative generates a surplus — an additional income stream that doesn't exist on other platforms.
Will PaDC actually launch? How do I know this is real?→
PaDC is already incorporated as a Pennsylvania worker cooperative, bylaws are complete, and 250+ drivers have engaged in the organizing process. We're actively building partnerships and securing funding ahead of our October 1, 2026 pilot launch. This is not a concept — it's an organization in motion.
How much does membership cost?→
Membership fee details are being finalized as part of PaDC's development process. Founding members — those who join during the current organizing phase — will receive priority access and the most favorable membership terms. Join the interest list now to be first in line when membership opens.
Can I still drive for Uber or Lyft while joining PaDC?→
Yes. Joining the interest list and becoming a PaDC member does not require you to stop driving for other platforms. Many drivers will continue working across multiple platforms as PaDC builds its ridership base after launch.
What are my rights as a member?→
Every PaDC member has one equal vote on all major cooperative decisions — including platform fees, governance policies, and leadership elections. Members also have the right to review PaDC's financials, participate in annual meetings, and share in cooperative surpluses through patronage dividends.
What if I want to leave? Can I get my membership back?→
Cooperative membership terms — including withdrawal and equity redemption policies — are defined in PaDC's bylaws and will be shared with all prospective members before they commit. PaDC is designed to be fair and transparent at every stage.
When does PaDC launch?→
PaDC's pilot launch target is October 1, 2026. Founding members recruited in the current phase will be the first drivers operating on the platform. Join the interest list to receive updates on our exact timeline as we get closer to launch.
Who can join PaDC?→
PaDC is currently focused on recruiting Philadelphia-area rideshare drivers. You don't need to be currently driving — if you have rideshare experience and are interested in the cooperative model, we want to hear from you. Add your name to the interest list and we'll be in touch.
