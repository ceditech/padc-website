Our story

Why PaDC was created

Philadelphia has thousands of rideshare drivers — working long hours, navigating a city they know better than anyone, keeping people moving. For years, they've been told that flexibility is the benefit. That being your own boss is the reward.

But drivers didn't build that story. They built the platform. And while the platform grew into a billion-dollar industry, drivers watched their earnings shrink, their fees increase, and their ability to influence anything about their work disappear entirely.

PaDC was formed because a group of Philadelphia drivers decided that a different model was not only possible — it already existed. Worker-owned cooperatives have been operating successfully for decades. The cooperative model has simply never been applied to rideshare at scale in Philadelphia.

PaDC exists to change that. Not as a protest. Not as a workaround. As a genuine, long-term alternative that puts drivers in the owner's seat — literally and economically.

What we believe

Mission & Vision

Create a driver-owned cooperative that builds economic opportunity for everyone who drives.

To create a driver-owned transportation cooperative that provides safe, reliable, and community-centered mobility services while building economic opportunity, ownership, and wealth for drivers and the communities they serve.

How it works

How a driver cooperative works

PaDC operates on the same cooperative principles used by successful worker-owned businesses across North America. The model is proven. The opportunity is yours.

01

Driver Joins

A driver pays a one-time membership fee — not a subscription, not a platform cut. A real ownership stake in the cooperative.

02

Driver Becomes an Owner

From day one, members hold an equity share in PaDC. You're not a contractor. You're not a gig worker. You're a co-owner.

03

Driver Has a Vote

Every member has an equal vote on platform fees, policies, leadership, and major decisions. One member, one vote — always.

04

Driver Shares in Success

When PaDC generates a surplus, it's returned to driver-members as patronage dividends — not sent to shareholders elsewhere.

Why Philadelphia

PaDC is Philadelphia — and Philadelphia needs PaDC.

Philadelphia is one of the most economically unequal large cities in the United States. Thousands of residents rely on rideshare income — yet every dollar extracted by corporate platforms leaves the city, weakening the communities where our drivers live and work.

Our progress

Organizational timeline

PaDC is moving systematically through the stages of cooperative development. Here's where we've been and where we're headed.

Early 2025
Driver organizing begins
Philadelphia-area drivers begin meeting to explore the cooperative model as an alternative to existing rideshare platforms.
March 2025
Bylaws completed
Cooperative bylaws drafted and finalized, establishing democratic governance, member voting rights, and patronage provisions.
June 2025
Articles of incorporation filed
PaDC formally established as a worker cooperative under Pennsylvania law.
2025
Community outreach & driver engagement
Driver information meetings held across Philadelphia. 250+ drivers engaged in organizing process.
Now — In Progress
Partnership & funding development
Active conversations with cooperative development organizations, economic development stakeholders, and funders to support pilot operations.
2026 — Upcoming
First driver-member cohort recruited
Recruit and onboard PaDC's founding cohort of driver-members ahead of pilot launch.
October 1, 2026
Pilot launch — Philadelphia
PaDC's driver-owned rideshare platform launches in Philadelphia with its founding member cohort.
Beyond 2026
Expansion & growth
Scale the cooperative model, grow driver-membership, and demonstrate that driver ownership can reshape the economics of rideshare.
