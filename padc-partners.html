The opportunity

A scalable model for community wealth building in the gig economy.

Philadelphia has thousands of rideshare drivers — working every day to keep the city moving, while watching their earnings shrink and their economic security erode. Corporate rideshare platforms extract hundreds of millions of dollars from local labor each year, with profits flowing out of Philadelphia to distant shareholders.

PaDC offers a structurally different model. By organizing drivers as co-owners of the platform they operate, PaDC creates a local economic institution that keeps wealth in Philadelphia, builds assets for working families, and demonstrates that gig economy work can be the foundation of real economic mobility.

This is the model that serious economic development funders, workforce development organizations, and community wealth-building advocates have been looking for — applied to one of the fastest-growing sectors of the urban economy.

Why PaDC matters

Six dimensions of community impact.

PaDC addresses interconnected challenges facing Philadelphia's working families — through a single, scalable cooperative infrastructure.

🏦

Local Wealth Retention

Every dollar kept from corporate platform extraction stays in driver households and Philadelphia neighborhoods — strengthening the local economy rather than extracting from it. PaDC is a mechanism for keeping gig economy earnings local.

📈

Economic Mobility

PaDC transforms gig work into an ownership pathway. Drivers don't just earn hourly income — they build equity, receive patronage dividends, and develop the financial skills and governance experience of real business ownership.

🛠️

Workforce Development

PaDC membership creates documented workforce development outcomes: business ownership skills, cooperative governance experience, financial literacy, and peer professional networks — outcomes measurable for workforce development funders.

🏘️

Small Business Creation

Every PaDC driver-member is a small business co-owner. PaDC's growth is measurable as small business development in Philadelphia — with the support infrastructure of a cooperative rather than the isolation of individual micro-entrepreneurship.

🚌

Transportation Access

Driver ownership creates genuine community accountability for service — including in neighborhoods chronically underserved by corporate rideshare platforms. PaDC aligns driver incentives with equitable transportation access.

🌱

Cooperative Infrastructure

PaDC builds the cooperative economy in Philadelphia. Each successful driver-owned business strengthens the broader ecosystem of worker ownership, democratic governance, and community economic institutions.
